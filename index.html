Welcome! Let's start with the course content.

In order to achieve our objective with the implementation of the Specific Recovery Model, which we told you about in the initial video, we must carry out the following actions:

  • Definition of productive structures

  • Definition of efficient Procedures and Processes

  • Design of activities based on automation

  • Implementation of control KPIs

  • Analysis of the risks associated with the processing of these types of case files

The details of these actions will be explained in the modules that make up this course, the titles of which you can see at the bottom of this Home page.

You should bear in mind that the scope of this recovery model is essentially aimed at the following:

  • At all countries whose companies conduct insurance business

  • At the Claims and Benefits Area, or those areas where the management of Recovery case files is concentrated

  • Focusing on the Automobiles line

Go to the Introduction contents now to learn about some more concepts.

We'll start with the Structure and Organization module.

In this module we will analyze the job positions we have in this Specific Recovery Model, as well as their functions and roles. In addition, we will also take a closer look at the knowledge of all the areas involved in recovery management.

Recovery management involves not only the Operations, Benefits and Providers Areas, but must work together with other Areas: Technical, Accounting/Cash/Financial, HR, and those with which we need to also establish relationship models.

Are you ready? You can access the various resources now.

The recovery process involves activities ranging from the analysis of the case file itself to the final allocation of the recovered amount. This is either 100 percent or the amount that corresponds to the criteria of fault and local management rules, as these should be the ones that establish the guidelines for whether or not to continue with a claim or move to legal proceedings.

In order to manage this correctly, we need to know the subprocesses that are included, as well as the various features of the tool, which will not differ from the basic needs in any other claim.

In this second module, we will discuss this.

We are now getting to know more about our Specific Recovery Model. We have seen what recovery consists of and that it must be managed by a specialist handler.

We are now going to look at the procedure to be followed in the management of this type of case file, always bearing in mind three aspects: operational management, financial management and client vision.

A very interesting module. You can access it now.

We´ve come to the fourth module of the course, in which we will deal with aspects related to the monitoring and supervision that we must carry out in the management of recoveries.

The purpose of establishing this monitoring is to gain detailed knowledge of the results of the different recovery management processes and to verify the effectiveness of the handling, ensuring that the procedures and activities carried out are adequate and are complied with in accordance with what has been established.

You can begin now.

We must rely on risk analysis when managing claims in order to improve internal monitoring, helping to prevent operational risk.

By operational risk, we mean the risk of loss arising from inadequate or dysfunctional internal processes, personnel or systems, or from external events, which shall include legal risks, but not risks arising from strategic decisions or reputational risks.

In this last module, we will learn how Claims Areas are involved in risk control procedures.
