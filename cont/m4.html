Among the management monitoring to be carried out by the coordinators of the handling team throughout the year, as indicated in the COM ( CLAIMS OPERATING MODEL), at least one on the management of recoveries should be considered. The following aspects must be analyzed as part of the technical monitoring:

  • Management indicators
  • Technical management of procedures
  • Management of Training and Resources
  • Management of agreements in accordance with the commitments undertaken therein
  • Quality management

Once the Management Monitoring Report has been closed and published, the person in charge of recovery management (when there is no specific department) will be responsible for making the required comments or action plans with regard to the aspects commented on. They must do so within 20 days of receiving the Report.

The Area Management and/or those in charge of performing the management monitoring shall, in the course of subsequent monitoring, review the results of the previous monitoring performed as well as the action plans that may have been drawn up, to confirm that the errors detected have been resolved.

Why is recovery case file management relevant to the company?

Because of the impact it can have on the main claims indicators

Correct management can lead to substantial savings for the company, both in the cost of the claims and in the decrease in associated management expenses (operational, legal).

And poor management can result in a negative reputational impact as well as higher incurred expenses.

Management indicators for recovery case files are specific items that help us measure the performance and success of the operation. The indicators make it possible to measure the degree of achievement of the objectives pursued.

It is therefore necessary to define and monitor the main indicators in this regard.

Want to know some of these indicators?

  • Recovery rate
  • Recovery efficiency
  • Recovery effectiveness
  • Expense-to-income ratio
  • Judicial rate
