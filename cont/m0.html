The concept of Recovery denotes the amount that MAPFRE has previously paid to its policyholder under a policy in force and that it recovers from the third party responsible for an accident or claim (or from its insurer).

The concept of conventional recovery is used to include recovery case files that are not associated with any type of agreement or arrangement between insurers and are handled in the usual way, as with any other type of claim.

In this type, there are essentially two situations: those in which MAPFRE recovers the amount paid on the basis of the coverage taken out for the company itself and those in which it is necessary to recover the specific amount that the policyholder has previously paid as a deductible.

Broadly speaking, collective agreements are sector-wide, bilateral or multilateral agreements between insurance companies. Their scope of action and content will depend on each agreement.

It should be noted that, within the agreements, the decision to take part in this type of sector-wide agreement is decisive, or not, depending on local circumstances. These circumstances must be analyzed prior to joining the agreement and periodically after the decision to join, in order to obtain optimal results in terms of efficiency and productivity. A company cannot be part of agreements where its results, in terms of efficiency, are inferior to conventional management.

The Agreements seek to improve efficiency by establishing common rules for all member companies and to ensure that all claims fall within the framework of the agreement, thereby reducing the time taken to handle claims.

The insurance company's Right of Recourse allows it to recover, from the policyholder, what was previously paid to the injured party in the cases determined by law or as the result of an agreement between the parties. The Right of Recourse is closely linked to local legislation and should therefore always be taken into consideration before initiating any Recourse proceedings.

The first requirement to be able to exercise the right of recourse is to have paid the corresponding indemnification to the injured third party as a consequence of an incident and provided that there is a legal cause or a condition by which the Company can be exempted from the corresponding indemnification, both to the policyholder and to third parties.

In this respect, we must always bear in mind the legal framework that covers this right in each country.
