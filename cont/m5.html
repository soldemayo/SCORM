Identification of risks

Identifying risks that affect claims processes and documenting established monitoring procedures in operations to prevent or mitigate those risks. These monitoring procedures will be identified in the respective internal monitoring manuals, which will provide the basic information for carrying out an analysis of the degree of effectiveness of the established monitoring and for designing the necessary action plans and corrective measures.

Participation in the process of analyzing risk indicators

Taking part in the process of analyzing risk indicators and controls under the coordination of the companies' Risk Management Areas. This will require the relevant personnel of the Claims Areas to complete the questionnaires. In this case, the Claims Areas will be involved in the preparation of the risk maps and the effectiveness of the controls used to understand and improve the status of the operation.

Establishing management and mitigation measures

The Claims Area, using the information obtained through the risk indicator maps and the criticality of each one, as well as the effectiveness of the defined monitoring, will establish the necessary management and mitigation measures for those risks with higher levels of criticality.

Any event or circumstance which, were it to occur (probability), would negatively impact (threat), to a greater or lesser extent (relative significance), the organization's capacity to achieve its operational and/or strategic objectives.

The dimensions for risk analysis are provided below:

  • Risk type
  • Affected processes
  • Description of the risk
  • Modality
  • Control
  • Mitigation measures
  • KRIs

Some examples of specialty risks for recoveries

  • Communications in recovery management are not completed correctly and in due time, as established in both internal and external regulations.
  • Personnel involved in the recovery process do not have the necessary experience to carry out the functions described in the specific recovery process.
  • There is no correct integration of all management applications (issuing, portfolio, etc.) for the correct management of recoveries.
  • The rating systems for recovery case files are inadequate.
