Definition

Opening

The concept of Recovery denotes the amount that MAPFRE has previously paid to its policyholder under a policy in force and that it recovers from the third party responsible for an accident or claim (or from its insurer).

These case files must be managed by specialist handlers, provided that the volume and local structure so permits.

They must be opened as soon as we know that there is a possibility of recovery; to do this, we must at least have identified the person responsible for the damage caused to our policyholder and liability for the accident, although this may be shared, with the fault not necessarily falling on one of the parties. Whenever possible, we must open these case files from capture (except for Recourse Recoveries, as these will be opened when the company identifies that it has incurred costs that must be borne by the policyholder themselves under the general and special conditions of their policy).

The recovery case files will be associated with an own coverage case file, for which MAPFRE assumes payment of the circumstances associated with the event.

Before going in depth into the recovery procedure, it is necessary to identify the situations in which we may need to open these case files.

They must be opened as soon as we know that there is a possibility of recovery.

Whenever possible, we must open these case files from capture (except for recourse recoveries, which will be opened as soon as we have evidence to confirm possible direct action against our policyholder).

In order to recover, we need to at least identify the third party at fault for the reported incident. Without their correct identification, we would not be able to recover.

In the case of Recoveries, as with other case files, we must ensure that our handlers possess extensive knowledge and awareness of the scope of coverage. These case files must be managed by specialist handlers.

The generation of a recovery case file should preferably be automatic as part of the usual capture process, observing a series of business rules or milestones, which each country can identify.

Recovery case files must contain the following financial information:

The collection expectation items must be associated with the collection or indemnification items detailed in the Claims Operating Model.

Reserve items must be associated with the expense items included in the Claims Operating Model.

Collection expectation

The amount at which the case file is assessed, based on the amount paid in the payment case file. Generally, this assessment has already been carried out for the amount reserved in the payment case file, therefore the data should automatically be entered in the payment case file.

If there is any financial change in the reserves of the payment case file (extension of damages), this should be automatically reflected in the collection expectations of the Recovery case file.

Reserve

Amount to consider and to cover possible expenses that may arise during the handling (payment to an appraiser, attorney, etc.).

Whether the generation of case files is automatic or manual, the times at which they must be generated, the profiles that can generate them, the steps to follow and all the aspects that must be taken into consideration, such as the documentation required, deadlines for the steps/tasks, interruption of the statute of limitations, closure of the case files, communication of refusals, collection expectations, reserves, economic movements, etc., must be included in a local recoveries procedure...

as is also indicated in the Claims Operating Model when a specific procedure is involved.

As a general guideline, before initiating management of this type of case file and opening the claim, it is necessary to analyze the approach we are intending to defend regarding the claim, in order to obtain compensation for the damages paid by the company.
For this, it is very important:

To analyze all the circumstances of the incident

Discuss the documentation or available media that support the claim

Broadly speaking, the recovery handler should pay close attention to the following aspects:

Capture information

verifying that we have sufficient and correct information from the capture.

Related case files

Verifying the case files associated with the claim and that the handling to be initiated will be carried out with the appropriate case files.

Obtaining further information

If more information is needed than we have, we will even request it directly from the policyholder.

Once we receive the amount in MAPFRE's accounts from the responsible party, the third-party company or our own policyholder (recourse recoveries), the collection must automatically be allocated to the correct case file, to the necessary items and, in turn, all expenses that may have been incurred must also be correctly allocated to the case file before it is closed.

The amount recovered must therefore be deducted from the total amount of the claim, so that when recovering 100 percent of the amount paid by the actual coverage case file, the cost of the claim will be zero.

When, for any reason, the amount of the claim is not reduced by 100 percent (contributory negligence), it will be reduced by the corresponding part according to the recovery made.

However, all other aspects to be taken into consideration for the management of recovery (deadlines, amounts, etc.) must be covered by a local procedure.
